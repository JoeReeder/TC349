Description of Process and Findings

The process I used for this website was that I first made the cards on index cards of all of the sections and pages of NASA's Moon website. Then, I did my sorting and grouping which is stated in the above table. After I sorted the index cards, three of my fraternity brothers also sorted the cards as I took notes.

From my experience with this website's card sorting exercise, I realized that, in my opinion, this website is very unorganized. It was very hard for me to grasp the concept of what each section had. Some of the sections that I had expected to have certain information (based upon the title given) did not have that information. Also, Images, Videos, and a variety of dates were sparatically placed throughout the entire website.

Much like my findings, my participants had similar groupings of the sections and pages. All particpants grouped the images and videos in one section, with their parent group being multimedia. The dates and events were split into their individual groupings and were grouped into a parent group along with the calendar. All facts were group together in the "About The Moon" section, and placed all of the missions along with the LADEE launch in a parent section of "Missions". Two of the three participants also added a "Contact" section to be more easily seen and changed the "Get Involved" section name to a more accurate name such as "Fun Related Activities", since it did not as much pertain to getting involved with the NASA program and more with projects and exibits to see.

I felt that the sorting of my participants was a better representation of how unorganized this site was. My opinion was biased with the fact that I had previously seen the site and leaned more towards the original organization structure. However, both my participants and I came to very similar conclusions.
